Depression Counselling

Depression can feel like carrying a heavy bag that nobody else can see. It may bring sadness, low energy, less interest in things, slower thinking, or a feeling that even small tasks are hard. This explains the issue in a simple and compassionate way.

The main message is that support is available. Counseling helps a person understand what is happening, how daily habits may be affected, and what small steps can slowly bring balance back.

Who this service helps

  • People with long-lasting low mood
  • People who have lost interest or motivation
  • People who feel emotionally tired or numb
  • People who need help rebuilding daily rhythm

Simple explanation

Think of the mind like a phone battery that is draining too fast. Counselling helps identify what is draining it and how to recharge it in a healthier way. That may include routine, sleep, support, and clearer thinking.

Some people prefer face-to-face sessions, while others need online support because it is easier to attend consistently. Both options can work well when the process is steady.
